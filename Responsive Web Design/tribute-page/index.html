Mzee Jomo Kenyatta

Freedom fighter, first prime minister and later president of Kenya

  • 1891Born in Gatundu, British East Africa
  • 1908Kenyatta left home and enrolled as a pupil at the Church of Scotland Mission (CSM) at Thogoto
  • 1920 He married Grace Wahu, under Kikuyu customs. When Grace got pregnant, his church elders ordered him to get married before a European magistrate, and undertake the appropriate church rites.
  • 1922Kamau began working, as a store clerk and water-meter reader for the Nairobi Municipal Council Public Works Department, once again under John Cook who was the Water Superintendent.Meter reading helped him meet many Kenyan-Asians at theirhomes who would become important allies later on.
  • 1929The KCA sent Kenyatta to London to lobby on its behalf with regard to Kikuyu tribal land affairs
  • 1963Kenyatta became prime minister of the autonomous Kenyan government. After independence, Queen Elizabeth II remained as Head of State
  • 1988Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota, thanks to a Depression
